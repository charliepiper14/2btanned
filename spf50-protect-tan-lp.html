Good to know

Your SPF questions, answered

Can you tan through sunscreen (SPF)?+

Yes. Sunscreen with SPF reduces UV damage but does not block all UV rays, so you can still develop a gradual tan while protecting your skin from sunburn and premature ageing.

What SPF should I use for tanning?+

If you burn easily or it is peak sun, reach for SPF50. For lighter days or a faster glow, SPF15 is ideal. The mist feels exactly the same either way, so it comes down to the protection you need.

Can you put SPF over fake tan?+

Yes. Let your tan develop and dry fully first, then mist SPF over the top to protect both your skin and your colour.

Does higher SPF completely block tanning?+

No. A higher SPF slows down UV exposure, but you will still develop a gradual, safer tan while staying better protected.

How often should I reapply while tanning?+

Every two hours as a benchmark, and always after swimming, towelling off or heavy sweating, since the mist is water-resistant rather than waterproof.
